## Summary

This governance action requests an incremental treasury withdrawal of **₳1,768,167**, equivalent to approximately **$424,360 USD** at the proposal’s stated estimate of **$0.24 per ada**, to upgrade Cardano’s already-ratified TOKEN2049 Singapore 2026 sponsorship from the Baseline “Platinum” tier to the “Title” tier.

The underlying Baseline “Platinum” sponsorship proposal was ratified before RCADA had the opportunity to vote on it. RCADA therefore does not treat this vote as retrospective endorsement of the Baseline decision. Instead, this review is limited to the incremental upgrade now before DReps.

RCADA votes **YES** on this top-up with reservations. The proposal is modular, the underlying sponsorship has already been approved by Cardano governance, and the Title upgrade provides defined additional benefits including a larger booth footprint, a 15-minute mainstage keynote, press release inclusion, facilitated media introductions, lanyard branding, a high-traffic engagement point, and additional event access.

RCADA’s support is cautious because there is limited hard evidence that premium conference sponsorship reliably produces proportional return on investment, especially during weaker market conditions. However, the proposal includes defined KPIs, refund provisions, surplus-return language, smart contract administration, third-party assurance, and public reporting commitments. These safeguards make a cautious YES reasonable, provided EMURGO and Intersect follow through with transparent post-event reporting.

- The proposal contains clear refund language if the top-up cannot be used as intended.

- Current weaker market conditions may dampen enthusiasm, investor appetite, and near-term conversion, but visible ecosystem presence during such periods may still have strategic value if executed well.
- RCADA expects post-event reporting to distinguish, as far as reasonably possible, the incremental outcomes of the Title upgrade from the already-approved Baseline sponsorship.

## What this action does

This action funds a top-up from the already-ratified TOKEN2049 Singapore 2026 Baseline “Platinum” sponsorship to a “Title” sponsorship.

The proposal requests **₳1,768,167**, stated as **$424,360 USD**, to secure the additional benefits associated with the Title tier. These include:

- expanding the booth footprint from 108 sqm to 144 sqm;
- securing a 15-minute mainstage keynote;
- inclusion in an event press release;
- facilitated introductions to media;
- lanyard branding;
- a branded protein shake station;
- additional invitations to the Speakers Reception and AFTER 2049;
- additional general event tickets;
- increased visibility for Cardano ecosystem projects participating at the event.

The proposal is explicitly dependent on the Baseline “Platinum” sponsorship. If the top-up were ratified without the Baseline sponsorship being approved, EMURGO committed to returning the funds to the Cardano Treasury. Since the Baseline proposal has now ratified, the practical question is whether the incremental Title upgrade is justified.

## Analysis Findings

### Constitutional / Guardrails Assessment

- ✔ The action is correctly framed as a Treasury Withdrawal.
- ✔ The proposal states the purpose of the withdrawal: funding a Title Sponsorship upgrade for TOKEN2049 Singapore 2026.
- ✔ The proposal identifies the delivery period: TOKEN2049 Singapore 2026, scheduled for October 7–8, 2026.
- ✔ The proposal includes a cost breakdown for the incremental upgrade.
- ✔ The proposal identifies refund conditions, including failure of the Baseline dependency, ada price appreciation surplus, event cancellation, and major disruption scenarios.
- ✔ The proposal includes administrative arrangements involving EMURGO, Intersect, CDH, smart contracts, third-party assurance, and public dashboarding.
- ✔ The proposal states that funds held in the treasury reserve and project-specific smart contracts will not be staked with an SPO and will be delegated to the auto-abstain DRep.
- ⚠ The full requested amount is released under a single milestone, which may be commercially practical for securing the sponsorship but provides limited milestone-based treasury control.
- ⚠ The main constitutional concern is whether the incremental spend is reasonable under Tenet 8, given that the added value is largely premium visibility and marketing exposure rather than durable infrastructure.
- ⚠ The proposal’s claimed benefits are plausible but may be difficult to attribute directly to the Title upgrade rather than the already-approved Baseline presence.

**Assessment:** Conditional Pass

The proposal appears to satisfy the main structural requirements expected of a Treasury Withdrawal under the Constitution. RCADA’s concern is not that the proposal is constitutionally defective, but that a premium marketing upgrade requires careful scrutiny under the principles of reasonable resource use, accountability, and public benefit.

---

### Process & Governance Quality

The proposal is stronger than many large spending actions because it is modular. DReps are not forced to support a premium Title-tier event package in order to support Cardano’s basic presence at TOKEN2049. The Baseline and top-up were separated, allowing the community to make a clearer decision on the value of premium marketing exposure.

RCADA values this structure. It directly addresses prior concerns about bundling and all-or-nothing treasury proposals.

The proposal also contains several governance-quality strengths:

- clear statement that the top-up depends on the Baseline sponsorship;
- explanation of what incremental benefits are unlocked;
- budget breakdown limited to the delta between Platinum and Title;
- no additional management fee for EMURGO on the top-up;
- Intersect administration;
- legal agreement with CDH;
- third-party assurance;
- public dashboard commitment;
- smart contract-based fund management;
- post-event reporting commitment.

However, there are still governance limitations.

The proposal relies on a single immediate release of the full top-up amount. That may be necessary to secure the sponsorship tier, but it reduces staged accountability. The KPI framework is useful, but the community will need to see whether reported results are meaningful, verifiable, and connected to follow-through rather than simply counting event activity.

RCADA also notes that it did not have the opportunity to vote on the Baseline proposal before it ratified. This creates an unusual decision environment: the core sponsorship is already approved, and RCADA is now assessing whether to improve an already-funded event presence rather than deciding whether the event presence should exist in the first place.

**Assessment:** Strong / Mixed

The modular structure and reporting commitments are strong. The single-release milestone and uncertainty around incremental attribution remain weaknesses.

---

### Impact & Risk Analysis

The potential upside is clear. TOKEN2049 Singapore is a major global Web3 event, and a Title-level presence may help Cardano present itself as a serious, active, builder-focused ecosystem. The additional mainstage keynote and media access could help Cardano reach investors, enterprises, builders, journalists, and ecosystem partners that may not otherwise engage deeply with Cardano.

The proposal is strongest where it supports ecosystem builders. Providing Cardano projects with a higher-profile booth environment, presentation opportunities, media exposure, and networking access may create value beyond simple brand marketing. If executed well, this could produce longer-term benefits through partnerships, content assets, user acquisition, and visibility for projects building on Cardano.

The main risk is that the additional spend may not produce proportional return. The top-up is expensive, and many of the incremental benefits are high-level visibility assets: keynote access, lanyards, press release inclusion, media introductions, and a larger booth. These benefits have strategic value, but they are harder to measure than infrastructure delivery, development milestones, or direct public-good outputs.

Market conditions are another risk. In a weaker market, investor appetite and conference conversion may be lower. Attendees may be more cautious, projects may have tighter budgets, and media enthusiasm may be more selective. This could dampen near-term ROI.

At the same time, bear-market visibility can be useful. Ecosystems that continue to show up professionally during quieter periods may gain credibility, especially if competitors reduce their activity. The value depends heavily on execution quality: the keynote must be meaningful, the booth must showcase real builders, media introductions must be followed up, and leads must be routed to relevant ecosystem participants.

RCADA therefore views this as a potentially useful but not guaranteed investment.

**Assessment:** Medium Risk / Moderate-to-Strong Potential Value

The risk is acceptable only if the proposer delivers transparent post-event reporting and demonstrates that the upgrade created measurable ecosystem benefit beyond general prestige.

## RCADA Rationale

RCADA votes YES on this top-up with reservations. We did not have the opportunity to vote on the Baseline Platinum proposal before it ratified, so this decision should not be interpreted as retrospective endorsement of that original action. Our review is limited to the incremental Title upgrade now before us.

Given that the Baseline sponsorship has already been approved by Cardano governance, this top-up presents a modular decision on whether the ecosystem should secure additional premium visibility at TOKEN2049 Singapore 2026. The upgrade provides a larger booth footprint, mainstage keynote, press release inclusion, media introductions, lanyard branding, and additional event access. These are tangible sponsorship benefits, and the proposal includes defined KPIs, refund provisions, surplus-return language, smart contract administration, third-party assurance, and public reporting commitments.

RCADA’s support is cautious. We do not see strong public precedent proving that premium conference sponsorship reliably produces proportional ROI, and current bear-market conditions may dampen enthusiasm, investor appetite, and near-term conversion. However, visibility during weaker market conditions can also be strategically useful if executed well, especially where builders receive meaningful access and Cardano presents a unified, professional ecosystem presence.

Our YES vote is therefore conditional in spirit: EMURGO and Intersect should provide clear post-event reporting, including evidence of builder participation, media outcomes, lead quality, partnership follow-through, wallet or user-acquisition results, and a transparent accounting of any surplus or unused funds. This vote should not establish a default precedent for high-cost event branding, but supports this specific modular upgrade following the already-ratified Baseline sponsorship.

## Post-Vote Monitoring Notes

RCADA should monitor whether the proposer provides:

- confirmation that funds were used only for the Title-tier upgrade and associated approved costs;
- clear evidence of builder participation and selection process;
- post-event report within the stated reporting period;
- breakdown of leads, media introductions, press coverage, wallet sign-ups, and partnership follow-through;
- distinction between Baseline outcomes and Title-upgrade incremental outcomes where possible;
- confirmation of any surplus returned to the Treasury;
- public dashboard availability and on-chain traceability of fund movements;
- disclosure of any unavoidable vendor commitments if the event is disrupted or cancelled.
